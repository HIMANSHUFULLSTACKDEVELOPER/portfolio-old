Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Early Life

Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India

Died: July 27, 2015, in Shillong, Meghalaya, India

Dr. Kalam came from a humble background and started working at an early age to supplement his family's income.

Career

  • 1960: Joined DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation)
  • 1969: Transferred to ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)
  • 1980s: Developed Agni and Prithvi missiles
  • 1990s: Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister

Presidency

Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007

Known as the "People's President" due to his connection with the youth and his inspirational speeches

Awards and Honors

  • 1981: Padma Bhushan
  • 1990: Padma Vibhushan
  • 1997: Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian honor)
  • 2013: Von Braun Award

Notable Books

  1. Wings of Fire: An Autobiography
  2. Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India
  3. India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium
  4. Turning Points: A Journey Through Challenges

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam will always be remembered for his contributions to India's scientific community and his inspirational leadership.
